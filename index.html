Javier Parada is a cybersecurity researcher specializing in Cyber Threat Hunting, Adversary Emulation, and Threat Intelligence, with a focus on defending critical infrastructures. His Ph.D. research explores proactive detection and analysis of advanced adversaries targeting OT/ICS environments by integrating adversary emulation and intelligence-driven hunting methodologies. His work leverages frameworks such as MITRE ATT&CK, CALDERA, and Atomic Red Team to model attacker behaviors, validate detection mechanisms, and enhance defensive resilience. Additionally, he investigates threat data correlation using NLP and graph-based models to infer adversary campaigns and infrastructure, contributing to intelligence-led defense strategies and improved situational awareness across critical sectors.
